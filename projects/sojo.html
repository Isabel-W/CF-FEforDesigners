Social Journal

About the project

The Social Journal app is a React Native-based mobile application designed to help users reflect on their use of digital technologies during one-on-one social interactions. As part of my master thesis, I adapted an existing social media diary app, transforming it into a shared platform for paired users to track device usage and stay mindful during in-person interactions.
The app provides nudges such as conversation starters, activity suggestions, and daily practices to encourage meaningful engagement and active participation in conversations. It aims to foster better connection and focus during social moments. The implementation included a two-week in-the-wild study on Android smartphones, offering valuable insights into user behavior and interaction patterns.
Through this project, I deepened my expertise in interaction design, user research, and app development while exploring innovative ways to promote digital well-being.
