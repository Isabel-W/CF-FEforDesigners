IMAP

About the project

This project aimed to digitize the recording of assembly work for electricity, gas, water, district heating, and cooling sectors. It included tour planning, preparation, rework, and the handover to digital archives.
The solution involved developing a progressive web app using OutSystems, focusing on a tablet-first approach for field workers. It also provided a planning tool for desktop users and tailored forms for mobile workers. A pre-study identified key pain points, driving the project’s focus on digitizing forms throughout their lifecycle—from planning to handover.
Duration: 11/2023 – 07/2025
Project Setup: Small agile project team
My Role: Head of UI/ UX

Diary Study

As part of the pre-study, a diary study was conducted to explore the feasibility of using cargo bikes as an alternative to current vehicles. The aim was to evaluate if these bikes could provide more physical exercise during the workday while testing their practicality for daily tasks, including managing limited cargo space and navigating potentially long routes.

Overcoming Process Complexity and Stakeholder Alignment

The first challenge was untangling sector-specific processes, many of which relied on unsuitable workarounds. Identifying stakeholders' actual goals required stepping back from existing workflows, which was initially difficult for some users.
Through workshops and iterative steps with high-fidelity prototypes, we demonstrated the potential for process improvement. This led to a streamlined workflow for preparation, execution, and rework, transforming skeptics into power users and showcasing the success of the approach.

Design Guide

Using OutSystems, we found the provided library and design file overly complex, with excessive colors and styles but missing key icons and components. To address this, I developed a tailored design library with a customized color and font scheme, a dedicated icon set, and reusable components for forms.
The components were scalable for both mobile and desktop, ensuring consistent design across all sectors. This approach simplified archiving, ensured equal treatment for all sectors, and significantly accelerated implementation.
